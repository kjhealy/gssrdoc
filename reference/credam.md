# Americans' credit score has to do with choices or things out of their control

credam

## Source

General Social Survey https://gss.norc.org

## Details

Question Now, thinking about Americans in general, do you think credit
scores are more the result of choices people make or things out of their
control? NOTE: This variable originates on the GSS Next follow-on, and
should not be analyzed without the GSS Next-specific weights WTSSPS_NEXT
or WTSSNRPS_NEXT

## Values

- `[1]` entirely choices they make

- `[2]` mostly choices they make

- `[3]` both choices they make and things out of their control

- `[4]` mostly things out of their control

- `[5]` entirely things out of their control

- `[NA(d)]` can't choose `[NA(i)]` iap `[NA(j)]` I don't have a job
  `[NA(m)]` dk, na, iap `[NA(n)]` no answer `[NA(p)]` not imputable
  `[NA(r)]` refused `[NA(s)]` skipped on web `[NA(u)]` uncodeable
  `[NA(x)]` not available in this release `[NA(y)]` not available in
  this year `[NA(z)]` see codebook
